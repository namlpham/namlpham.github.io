About Me

My name is Nam Pham (he/him). I am a research analyst and climate communicator based in Lenapehoking, aka New York City. My work focuses on the exploration and implementation of sustainable climate visions through community engagement, policy analysis, strategic programming, art, design, and creative story-telling.

I am currently the Community Outreach and Engagement Specialist at the Center for Engagement, Environmental Justice, and Health (CEEJH, Inc.) where I provide technical assistance and capacity-building support to grassroots, frontline, and fenceline communities across the Mid-Atlantic and Northeast regions to address environmental, climate, and energy challenges.

I am also a contracted Climate Policy Researcher for the Indigenous Environmental Network since 2022, where I research and analyze policies and initiatives related to climate change, climate finance, and carbon management.

In 2023, I served as a lead researcher that worked on the development of a data synthesis framework aimed to understand the comparative performance of Natural and Nature-Based Features in New York State. This project was funded by NY Department of State and led by the Science and Resilience Institute at Jamaica Bay. During my time there, I got the opportunity to work with a diverse group of stakeholders spanning across different sectors, including NYSDOS, NYC Parks, NY Sea Grant, local permitters, research institutes, engineering and architecture firms, community partners, among others.

I hold a Master of Science in Environmental Policy and Sustainability Management from The New School. I was also a member of BIPOC Earth, an Environmental Justice Collective Student Organization, and Urban Systems Lab, interdisciplinary research, design, and practice space at the university.

In my free time, I enjoy spending time with friends and family, drawing on my iPad, playing Nintendo games, cooking Vietnamese food, coming up with art projects, and reading thought-provoking books. I am currently reading Dimensions of Citizenship: Architecture and Belonging from the Body to the Cosmos and I definitely recommend it.

My Approach

Systems Approach

I am deeply inspired by systems thinking and committed to incorporating a systems approach to address the complex issues I face in my work.

Co-Creation

I employ participatory co-creation methodologies with a focus on relationship building and power balance analysis to ensure that processes, solutions, and communications are inclusive, equitable, and just.

Creative Problem Solving

I apply creative problem-solving, strategic planning through game theory, and evidence-based frameworks like logic models to design impactful, community-driven projects and initiatives.
